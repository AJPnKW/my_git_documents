Fit And Motivation

Concise Direct

I bring long-running software and enterprise application delivery experience, including health and benefits work at Sun Life and developer-facing release delivery at Manulife. This role fits because it combines structured project leadership, technical coordination, and meaningful healthcare software impact in Waterloo.

Strategic Managerial

My background is strongest where software delivery needs both execution discipline and technical credibility. I have led projects across scope, schedule, budget, risk, reporting, vendor coordination, and cross-functional delivery, and I see this role as a strong fit because Venuiti needs a project manager who can keep an established healthcare application moving in a controlled, stakeholder-aware way.

Warmer Conversational

What pulls me to this role is that it sits at the intersection of two things I like most: organized software delivery and work that actually matters to people. I have managed complex technology projects for a long time, and the chance to bring that experience into healthcare, in a team close to home, is genuinely appealing.

Project Delivery

Concise Direct

A strong example is Sun Life, where I led multimillion-dollar application and Group Benefits initiatives. I built integrated plans across scope, time, cost, risk, communication, and procurement, coordinated multiple teams, and kept delivery controlled through execution.

Strategic Managerial

When I lead software delivery, I start by defining decision paths, scope boundaries, milestones, dependencies, and reporting expectations early so execution stays stable later. At Sun Life, that meant running high-visibility application work with disciplined planning, stakeholder alignment, and controlled execution across multiple streams and teams.

Warmer Conversational

One of the better examples is from Sun Life. It was the kind of project where a lot of teams had to move together, so my job was to make sure the plan was clear, the tradeoffs were visible, and people stayed aligned as the work progressed.

Stakeholder Management

Concise Direct

I make priorities explicit, document tradeoffs, and bring the right people into the decision at the right level. In a Manulife recovery project, that helped align business sponsors, vendors, and IS governance without losing momentum.

Strategic Managerial

I separate opinion from impact by grounding conflicts in scope, risk, timing, and customer outcome. My role is to create enough structure that developers, business stakeholders, and partners can understand the tradeoff, then move the decision through the right governance path instead of letting conflict linger informally.

Warmer Conversational

Usually the first step is slowing the conversation down just enough to get everyone talking about the same problem. Once people can see what the tradeoff actually is, it becomes much easier to protect the team, keep trust with stakeholders, and make a practical decision.

Risk And Problem Solving

Concise Direct

I look for unclear ownership, unstable scope, missing governance, and unresolved dependencies early. In a Manulife outsourced project, I stepped in, imposed planning and governance discipline, and turned a loosely structured effort into controlled delivery with better customer outcomes.

Strategic Managerial

My approach is to identify whether the real issue is execution, structure, or decision latency. Once I know that, I put in the lightest control that will stabilize the work, because the goal is not process for its own sake; the goal is restoring predictability and reducing downstream cost.

Warmer Conversational

Most delivery problems start showing themselves before they become visible on a dashboard. I try to catch them when they still look small, usually through gaps in ownership or unclear decisions, and then deal with them directly before the team spends weeks working around them.

Agile And Process

Concise Direct

I use Agile as a way to improve feedback and adaptability, but I still keep scope, dependencies, reporting, and decision controls visible. Agile should increase delivery clarity, not reduce it.

Strategic Managerial

The balance is to preserve iterative delivery at the team level while maintaining management-level control over commitments, cross-team dependencies, risks, and change decisions. I am comfortable working in Agile environments as long as the operating model still supports accountability and predictable delivery.

Warmer Conversational

I like Agile when it helps teams learn and move, but I do not treat it as a reason to become vague. The team still needs clarity on what matters, what is changing, and who is making the hard calls.

Concise Direct

Strategic Managerial

Warmer Conversational

Technical Fluency

Strategic Managerial

Warmer Conversational

Documentation And Reporting

Concise Direct

I keep reporting focused on status, decisions, risks, dependencies, changes, and what needs attention next. Good documentation should make execution easier, not heavier.

Strategic Managerial

My reporting style is to give stakeholders enough visibility to make timely decisions while keeping the team aligned on facts rather than interpretations. In regulated environments, that discipline also helps with traceability, audit readiness, and smoother change control.

Warmer Conversational

I treat documentation as part of delivery, not as cleanup at the end. If the project is being run well, the important decisions, changes, and risks should already be visible in how the work is managed.

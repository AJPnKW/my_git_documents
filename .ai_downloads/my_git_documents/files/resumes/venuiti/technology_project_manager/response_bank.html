Tell me about yourself Why Venuiti Closing pitch

1. Tell Me About Yourself

Best default answer. Aim for 75 to 90 seconds.

Version A - Best overall

I have spent most of my career working where software delivery, enterprise systems, and real business operations meet. A lot of that work sat inside project or program roles, but it was rarely just schedule tracking. It involved coordinating developers, business leads, vendors, operational teams, and sometimes offshore resources to move application and platform work from plan to execution without losing control of scope, timing, or documentation.

The strongest examples for this role are probably Sun Life, Manulife, Hubble, Kuntz, and Spaenaur. At Sun Life and Manulife I managed application and business-systems initiatives in structured enterprise environments. At Hubble and Kuntz I was closer to ERP delivery, implementation, upgrades, training, change control, and client-facing execution. At Spaenaur I was leading applications, development, support, and portfolio priorities more broadly.

So even though some later roles leaned more into risk and compliance, the long pattern underneath has been technical delivery, systems thinking, and getting complex work across the line. That is a big part of why this role makes sense to me.

Version B - Slightly shorter

My background is a mix of technical project management, enterprise applications, ERP and systems delivery, and regulated-environment execution. Over time I have led software, application, and business-systems work across insurance, manufacturing, consulting, and technology environments. The common thread has been coordinating people, plans, issues, and deliverables so that the work actually lands.

The most relevant pieces for Venuiti are my application and software-delivery work at Sun Life and Manulife, my ERP consulting and implementation work at Hubble and Kuntz, and my broader IT leadership at Spaenaur.

2. Why Venuiti, and Why This Role?

Aim for 45 to 75 seconds.

Version A - Best overall

A few things stand out to me about Venuiti. First, the company is clearly close to the work. The way you talk about custom software, integration, testing, documentation, and support feels practical rather than overly corporate. Second, the healthcare angle matters. I like work that has a real use case and real end users behind it, and software that improves access, coordination, or day-to-day outcomes is more meaningful than building something abstract. Third, the size is appealing. In a smaller company, the work is usually closer to the product, the team, and the decisions, and that fits how I like to operate.

The role itself lines up with the part of my background I want to keep leaning into: technical project management in environments where software delivery, integrations, documentation, stakeholder coordination, and regulated requirements all matter at the same time. I think I bring delivery discipline and enough technical context to be useful here without adding unnecessary process overhead.

Version B - More compact

I am interested in Venuiti because the role sits at the intersection of software delivery, integrations, stakeholder coordination, and meaningful healthcare work. The company also feels smaller, more practical, and closer to the actual work than many larger environments, which is a better fit for how I like to operate. The position lines up well with my background in technical project delivery, enterprise applications, and regulated implementation work.

3. Closing Pitch

Use near the end if they ask why they should hire you, or as a closing summary.

Version A - Best overall

I think the value I bring here is a mix that fits the role well: strong delivery discipline, real technical PM experience, comfort with enterprise applications and integration-heavy work, enough regulatory depth to handle sensitive environments well, and a practical operating style that fits a smaller software company. I can bring structure, visibility, and follow-through without slowing the work down.

Version B - Slightly warmer

I think I would be a strong fit because the role needs someone who can help the work move, keep technical and business people aligned, and make sure the details hold together. That has been a repeating pattern in my career, and it is still the kind of work I enjoy doing.

  • Lead with TPM and software delivery, not risk/compliance.
  • Use Hubble, Kuntz, Sun Life, Manulife PM, and Spaenaur first.
  • Use OpenText and risk roles as credibility for regulated delivery, documentation, reporting, and structured follow-through.

Support Journey Map

This is the intended order for moving through the information.

Journey stageQuestion to answerWhy it mattersWhere to go
1. Current situationWhat support does he already have?Avoids repeating short-term supports that already failed.
2. ODSP workerIs the ODSP worker actively helping with employment supports?ODSP is already connected and may open faster referral paths.
3. DSO eligibilityIs DSO active, incomplete, or unknown?DSO may unlock developmental-service supports and Passport funding.
4. Job-retention supportDoes he need support after hiring?Repeated short employment points to workplace support, training, coaching, and fit.
5. Education repairDid college fail because supports were missing?Accessible Learning should be involved before another attempt.
6. Rights checkWere disability accommodation needs considered?Work and school may require reasonable accommodation planning.

DSO + Passport

Use this section to prevent support needs from being missed during intake or funding conversations.

AreaWhat it meansWhat to sayWhy it matters
DSO eligibilityAdult developmental services access point.“I need to confirm eligibility or update my application.”Eligibility may unlock Ministry-funded supports.
Application packageCaptures support needs and service requests.“Please help me include every relevant support area.”Understating needs can cause service gaps.
PassportReimbursement funding for eligible adults.“Can Passport support employment readiness, planning, support workers, or agency services?”May fund supports not covered by a short program.
Person-directed planningHelp building a practical life/work roadmap.“I need a plan for stable work and supports.”This matches the need for long-term direction.
DocumentationAssessment or records may be needed.“What documents do I need, and what if I do not have them?”Prevents the process from stopping at paperwork.
